Family Reunion Villa in Puerto Rico

A private 3-bedroom home in Maunabo, with the kind of pace where the kids meet their cousins at the pool, the abuelos take the porch, and dinner runs long every night.

The hardest part of a family reunion is usually the lodging. Hotels split the family across rooms and floors. Resorts force everyone into the same restaurant at the same dinner seating. Cruises put everyone on different decks.

A villa fixes most of that. Casa Chunan is a 3-bedroom luxury home in Maunabo, on the southeast coast of Puerto Rico, that families have been booking for week-long reunions, multi-generational trips, and milestone-birthday gatherings.

Why a Villa Beats a Hotel for Reunions

The math is simple, the experience is even better.

Cost

Casa Chunan is $172/night. Sleeps 6. That works out to $29 per person per night for a full family of 6. The same family in two adjoining hotel rooms in San Juan typically pays $300 to $500 per night. Over a 7-day reunion, the savings cover most of one airfare.

One kitchen

One full kitchen lets the family cook together — and it lets the family eat together at any hour. The grandparents who do not feel like a 9 PM restaurant meal can cook the way they always have. The teenagers who graze through the day can graze. The kids' breakfast does not depend on a hotel buffet schedule.

One living space

Three generations in one open-plan living-dining room beats three generations in three separate hotel rooms. The reunion happens in the shared space, not by appointment.

Quiet at night

Private bedrooms, private bathrooms, no shared hotel walls. When the kids fall asleep at 8, the adults can sit on the terrace and talk into the night.

Why Maunabo Specifically

Maunabo is on the southeast coast of Puerto Rico — a small coastal town that most visitors miss. That is the point.

Casa Chunan sits five minutes from Playa Los Bohíos, the local family beach. Conditions are usually gentle and protected, especially on calm days. The town has a couple of supermarkets, a handful of colmado restaurants, and the pace of a place that has not turned itself over to tourism.

For a family reunion, that translates to: easy parking, easy beach access, easy meals, and a kind of quiet that San Juan and Rincón do not have. The kids can run. The adults can decompress. Nobody is fighting cruise crowds.

A Sample 5-Day Reunion Schedule

This is the rhythm most families settle into. Adapt it to your own.

Day 1 — Arrival

  • Family lands at SJU on staggered flights through the day
  • Rented vehicles pick everyone up, 90-minute drive to Maunabo
  • Arrive at Casa Chunan, grocery run at the local supermarket
  • Welcome dinner at the villa — pizzas, takeout from a local colmado, or a casual pool barbecue

Day 2 — Beach day

  • Slow morning, breakfast on the terrace
  • Mid-morning at Playa Los Bohíos — the kids in the water, the adults under umbrellas
  • Late lunch at one of the beach kioskos
  • Afternoon nap or pool time at the villa
  • Dinner: family cooking together

Day 3 — Adventure day

  • Day trip to El Yunque rainforest (45 minutes north)
  • Or a Punta Tuna Lighthouse walk (10 minutes from Casa Chunan)
  • Or a bioluminescent bay tour from Fajardo (90 minutes north, evening)
  • Dinner at the villa or at a Maunabo seafood restaurant

Day 4 — Family day

  • Late breakfast at the villa
  • Group photos at the lighthouse or on the terrace at golden hour
  • Big family dinner — chef-prepared at the villa, or out at a local colmado
  • Toasts, dominoes, music, late evening on the terrace

Day 5 — Wrap and depart

  • Final group breakfast
  • One last beach swim
  • Drive back to SJU for evening flights
Most family reunions are about being together long enough that the conversations actually happen. Casa Chunan is built for that — kids in the pool, abuelos on the porch, dinner running until everyone is full.

FAQ: Family Reunions at Casa Chunan

How many family members can stay at Casa Chunan?

The villa has 3 private bedrooms and sleeps up to 6 overnight. For larger family reunions, ask the host about nearby Maunabo lodging options that can extend overall capacity — additional villas are not directly contracted but several are typically available in town.

Is Casa Chunan safe and easy for families with young kids?

Maunabo is a small, calm coastal town. Playa Los Bohíos, the local beach 5 minutes away, is the local family beach with generally gentle protected swimming conditions; always check ocean conditions on the day. The villa has a private pool — supervise young swimmers as you would at any vacation rental. Kid-friendly groceries are available at the local supermarket and at colmados within a 5-minute drive.

Can grandparents handle a stay at Casa Chunan?

Most do. The villa is air-conditioned throughout and is a 90-minute drive from San Juan International Airport. The pace of Maunabo is slow and walkable. For specific accessibility needs (single-level access, mobility considerations, etc.), confirm details directly with the host before booking.

What does a family reunion week typically cost at Casa Chunan?

A 7-night family reunion for 6 starts around $1,204 in lodging at the base $172/night rate (works out to $29 per person per night). Add flights, a rental SUV, groceries plus 4-5 dining-out meals, and a couple of group activities, and a typical reunion week runs $4,500 to $6,500 all-in for a family of 6 from the US East Coast.

Can the host arrange a chef or catered meals for a family reunion?

Kimlee can point you toward local private chefs, lechón vendors, and caterers — booking is then arranged directly with those vendors. Casa Chunan's full kitchen also makes self-catering easy for families who prefer to cook together.

What is the best time of year for a family reunion in Puerto Rico?

December through April is high season — dry weather, lower hurricane risk, and family-friendly festivals (parrandas in December, San Sebastian Street Festival in January). May, June, and November are sweet spots for lower prices with still-strong weather. Most family reunions choose either the holiday window or a long-weekend window in March-April or June.
