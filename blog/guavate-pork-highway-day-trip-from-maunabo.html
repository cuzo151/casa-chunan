Guavate Pork Highway Day Trip from Maunabo: Lechón, Mountain Roads, and a Villa Nightcap

By Kimlee Gely · Host at Casa Chunan · Published June 25, 2026

← All Posts

Some Puerto Rico trips are remembered by beaches. Others are remembered by the first bite of crispy cuerito. Guavate, Puerto Rico’s most famous pork highway, is one of the best food day trips to pair with a stay at Casa Chunan.

Discover Puerto Rico describes Guavate in Cayey as the island’s most well-known pork highway, where visitors drive Route 184 and find a parade of lechoneras serving slow-roasted pork, traditional sides, music, and a lively weekend atmosphere.1

Why Guavate belongs in a Casa Chunan itinerary

Casa Chunan sits on the quiet southeast coast, while Guavate sits inland in the mountains. Together they create the kind of day that makes Puerto Rico feel layered: Caribbean coastline in the morning, mountain food culture by lunch, and a private villa evening back in Maunabo.

Go with a group if possible. The official guide recommends ordering like a local with a combination plate, trying sides such as arroz con gandules, yuca al mojo, tostones, sweet plantains, and mofongo, and enjoying the communal atmosphere.1

When to go

For the liveliest atmosphere, go on Saturday or Sunday and aim to arrive before noon because crowds build quickly.1 For a quieter experience, visit during the week when the energy is calmer but the food can still be excellent.

What to order

Start with lechón and a few sides. If your group is adventurous, try morcilla, guineítos en escabeche, or whatever looks best in the cafeteria-style line. Popular Guavate lechoneras mentioned by the tourism guide include Lechonera Los Amigos, El Mojito, Doctor Lechón, Lechonera Los Pinos, El Rancho Nuevo, and El Rancho Original.1

How to balance the day

Guavate is not a polished resort excursion. It is loud, local, fragrant, and filling. That is why it pairs well with Casa Chunan. After the music and mountain roads, the villa gives everyone room to shower, relax, and let the day slow down again.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Pork Highway in Puerto Rico?

The main Pork Highway is Route 184 in Guavate, Cayey, where lechoneras serve slow-roasted pork and traditional sides.

When is the best time to visit Guavate?

Weekends before noon are best for the lively local atmosphere; weekdays are better for a quieter visit.

What should I order in Guavate?

Order lechón with sides such as arroz con gandules, yuca al mojo, tostones, sweet plantains, or mofongo.

Is Guavate good for families?

Yes, it can be a fun family food trip, especially for groups that enjoy casual dining, music, and local culture.

Can Guavate be a day trip from Casa Chunan?

Yes. It works well as a lunch-centered day trip from Maunabo, especially when you keep the evening relaxed.
