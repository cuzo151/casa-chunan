Puerto Rico Hurricane Season:
The Honest Travel Guide

By Kimlee Gely · Host at Casa Chunan, Maunabo · June 15, 2026 · 8 min read
Casa Chunan› Travel Guides› Hurricane Season Guide

Every year, the same question lands in my inbox around May: "We want to come in September — is that crazy?"

The honest answer is no. But it deserves more than a one-word reply. I've hosted guests at Casa Chunan through multiple hurricane seasons, and I've watched people make both smart and regrettable decisions about summer travel to Puerto Rico. This guide is what I tell every single one of them.

"Most days during hurricane season in Puerto Rico are sunny, warm, and completely beautiful. The risk is real — but so is the reward."

What Is Hurricane Season, Actually?

The Atlantic hurricane season officially runs from June 1 through November 30. But "hurricane season" is not a six-month storm — it's a six-month window of elevated probability. The vast majority of days within that window are perfectly fine for travel.

Here's the breakdown by month, based on historical NOAA data for Puerto Rico:

June
Low Risk

Season opens, but activity is minimal. Hot, occasional afternoon showers. Great beach weather.

July
Low Risk

Warm and mostly sunny. Fewer tourists. Prices drop noticeably from peak season.

August
Moderate

Activity picks up. Still mostly fine, but start watching NOAA forecasts 7–10 days out.

September
Peak Risk

Statistically the most active month. Book refundable everything. Travel insurance is non-negotiable.

October
Moderate

Risk decreases through the month. Late October is often excellent — warm, quiet, affordable.

November
Low Risk

Season winds down. Weather is beautiful. This is a hidden gem month for Puerto Rico travel.

The Real Upside: Why People Come Anyway

Off-season Puerto Rico is a different island. Not worse — different. The crowds that pack Condado and Old San Juan in January are gone. The beaches are quieter. The restaurants have tables. And the prices reflect all of this.

  • Flights: Round-trip from the US East Coast can be $100–200 cheaper than peak season.
  • Accommodations: Vacation rentals like Casa Chunan typically run 20–35% below winter rates.
  • Beaches: Playa Los Bohios and Playa El Niño in Maunabo are never crowded, but in summer they're practically private.
  • Local events: The Maunabo Crab Festival and other local celebrations happen in summer. This is when Puerto Rico is most Puerto Rico.

What Maunabo's Weather Actually Looks Like in Summer

Maunabo sits on Puerto Rico's southeast coast, one of the drier sides of the island. The northeast trade winds that bring rain to El Yunque largely bypass the southeast. Summer here means:

What to Do If a Storm Approaches

Storm Preparedness Checklist

  • Book refundable: Always use refundable rates for flights and accommodations during hurricane season.
  • Get travel insurance with hurricane coverage: Verify explicitly that your policy covers hurricane-related cancellations and evacuations.
  • Monitor NOAA: Bookmark nhc.noaa.gov — the National Hurricane Center. Check it daily starting 10 days before your trip during August–October.
  • Know the alert levels: Tropical Watch = possible conditions in 48 hours. Tropical Warning = conditions expected in 36 hours. Hurricane Watch/Warning = more serious. Follow local government instructions immediately.
  • Have an exit plan: Know the nearest airport (Luis Muñoz Marín in San Juan, or Mercedita in Ponce for southeast PR).
  • Contact your host: Good hosts will communicate proactively and help you make the right call.

My Honest Recommendation

June, July, and November are excellent months to visit Puerto Rico with minimal hurricane risk and maximum value. They're my personal favorites for guests who want the real island experience without peak-season prices or crowds.

August and October are fine with proper preparation — refundable bookings, travel insurance, and a willingness to monitor the forecast.

September is the one month I'd approach with the most caution. Many guests come and have wonderful trips, but it requires the most flexibility, preparation, and clear-eyed understanding of the risk.

Whatever month you choose, Maunabo will be here — quiet, beautiful, and unhurried in the way that only the southeast coast of Puerto Rico can be.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to visit Puerto Rico during hurricane season?

Generally yes. Most days are sunny and warm. The probability of a hurricane making landfall on any specific week is low. Buy travel insurance, monitor NOAA forecasts, and book refundable rates.

What months are hurricane season in Puerto Rico?

Officially June 1 through November 30. Peak risk is mid-August through mid-October, with September being statistically the most active month.

How much cheaper is Puerto Rico during hurricane season?

Airbnb and hotel rates typically drop 20–40% compared to peak winter season. Flights from the US East Coast are often $100–200 cheaper round-trip.

What should I do if a hurricane approaches during my trip?

Monitor NOAA's National Hurricane Center (nhc.noaa.gov). If a watch or warning is issued, follow local government evacuation orders. Book refundable flights and accommodations. Travel insurance with hurricane coverage is strongly recommended.
